The primary alkaloids in kratom interact with various receptor systems in the body, particularly opioid receptors. However, kratom's mechanism differs significantly from traditional opioids.

**Mitragynine**, the most abundant alkaloid in green vein kratom, acts as a partial agonist at mu-opioid receptors. This partial agonism means it activates these receptors less fully than traditional opioids, potentially explaining kratom's different safety profile.

**7-hydroxymitragynine**, present in smaller quantities, shows higher potency at opioid receptors but still functions differently than conventional opioid drugs.

Beyond opioid receptors, kratom alkaloids also interact with:
- Adrenergic receptors (involved in energy and alertness)
- Serotonin receptors (related to mood)
- Dopamine receptors (associated with motivation and pleasure)

This multi-receptor activity contributes to kratom's complex effects profile and explains why it produces both stimulating and relaxing effects depending on dose and strain.
